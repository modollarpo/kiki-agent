1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" (remaining on your device until deleted or expired) or "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser). They can also be "first-party" (set by KIKI) or "third-party" (set by other services we use).

2. How We Use Cookies

KIKI uses cookies and similar technologies for several purposes:

  • Essential Functionality: To enable core features of our platform (authentication, security, session management)
  • Performance and Analytics: To understand how users interact with our website and platform
  • User Preferences: To remember your settings and preferences
  • Security: To detect fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access
  • Marketing: To deliver relevant advertising and measure campaign effectiveness (with your consent)

4. Other Tracking Technologies

4.1 Web Beacons (Pixels)

We use web beacons (also called pixels or clear GIFs) in emails and on our website to track whether you've opened an email or visited certain pages. This helps us measure email campaign effectiveness and website engagement.

4.2 Local Storage

We use browser local storage to store larger amounts of data locally, such as:

  • User interface state (dashboard layouts, filter settings)
  • Cached data to improve performance
  • Temporary data for form auto-save functionality

You can clear local storage through your browser settings.

4.3 Session Storage

Session storage is used for temporary data that is cleared when you close the browser tab, such as:

  • Multi-step form progress
  • Temporary filters and search queries
  • Navigation state

4.4 Device Fingerprinting

For security and fraud prevention purposes, we may collect technical information about your device (browser type, screen resolution, operating system, installed fonts) to create a "fingerprint" that helps us detect suspicious activity and protect your account.

5. Your Cookie Choices and Controls

5.1 Cookie Consent Manager

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner that allows you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize cookie preferences by category

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our website footer or by visiting https://kiki.com/cookie-settings.

5.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies after each browsing session
  • View and delete individual cookies

Learn how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Cookie settings in Chrome
  • Firefox: Cookie settings in Firefox
  • Safari: Cookie settings in Safari
  • Edge: Cookie settings in Edge

5.3 Do Not Track (DNT)

KIKI respects Do Not Track (DNT) browser settings. If you have enabled DNT in your browser:

  • We will disable cross-site tracking

To enable DNT, visit your browser settings and turn on the "Do Not Track" or "Send Do Not Track" option.

6. CCPA and "Do Not Sell My Personal Information"

KIKI does NOT sell your personal information. We do not and will not sell personal information to third parties for monetary or other valuable consideration.

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the use of certain cookies and tracking technologies may be considered a "sale" or "sharing" of personal information. California residents have the right to opt out of such "sales."

To exercise your CCPA rights:

  • Use our cookie consent manager to reject marketing cookies
  • Enable the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal in your browser
  • Contact us at privacy@kiki.com with "CCPA Opt-Out" in the subject line

We honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals and will automatically opt you out of non-essential cookies when detected.

7. Cookies and Children's Privacy

Our platform is not directed to children under 16 (or 13 in the United States). We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information via cookies, please contact us at privacy@kiki.com.

8. International Data Transfers

Some of the cookies we use may result in the transfer of your personal information to countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA), such as the United States. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with third-party providers
  • Providers' participation in recognized privacy frameworks (e.g., EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework where applicable)
  • Additional technical and organizational security measures

For more information, see our Privacy Policy and Data Processing Agreement.
