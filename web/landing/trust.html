Incident Response

We maintain a comprehensive incident response program to quickly detect, respond to, and recover from security events.

üö® Incident Response Process

Phase 1: Detection (0-15 minutes)
Automated Alert & Triage
Security monitoring systems detect anomalies. Automated alerts sent to on-call security team. Initial severity assessment and incident categorization.
Phase 2: Response (15 minutes - 4 hours)
Containment & Investigation
Incident response team assembled. Affected systems isolated to prevent spread. Forensic investigation initiated. Stakeholders notified based on severity.
Phase 3: Remediation (4-24 hours)
Eradication & Recovery
Root cause identified and eliminated. Systems restored from clean backups if needed. Security controls strengthened. Affected customers notified per legal requirements.
Phase 4: Post-Incident (24-72 hours)
Analysis & Improvement
Post-incident review conducted. Lessons learned documented. Security controls updated. Process improvements implemented.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach affecting personal data, we will notify affected customers within 72 hours of discovery (GDPR Article 33 requirement). Notifications include incident details, affected data, impact assessment, and remediation steps. We maintain cyber insurance coverage for breach-related costs.

Vulnerability Disclosure

We welcome responsible disclosure of security vulnerabilities from the security research community.

üîê Responsible Disclosure Program

If you discover a security vulnerability in KIKI's systems, we encourage you to report it responsibly. We commit to working with security researchers to verify and address legitimate vulnerabilities.

How to Report:

  • Email: security@kiki.com (PGP key available)
  • PGP Fingerprint: 4A3E 8B2C 9D1F 7E5A 6C8B 3F2E 1D9A 5C7B 8E4F 2A1C
  • Bug Bounty: We offer rewards for qualifying vulnerabilities (details upon contact)

What to Include:

  • Detailed description of the vulnerability
  • Steps to reproduce the issue
  • Potential impact and exploit scenario
  • Your contact information for follow-up

Our Commitment:

  • Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
  • Provide regular updates on remediation progress
  • Credit researchers who wish to be acknowledged (with permission)
  • Never pursue legal action against good-faith researchers

Out of Scope:

  • Denial of Service (DoS) attacks
  • Social engineering attacks against employees
  • Physical attacks against our facilities
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party services we use (report to them directly)
