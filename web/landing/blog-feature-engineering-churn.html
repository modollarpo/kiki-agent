Feature Engineering for Churn Prediction: 50+ Behavioral Signals

December 22, 2025 • 11 min read • By Alex Lee, Principal ML Engineer

Our canonical churn feature set spans engagement, payments, product adoption, support signals, and social proof to maximize lead time and recall.

← Back to Blog

Signal Families

Engagement

DAU/WAU/MAU ratios, session depth, dwell time, scroll velocity, recency decay.

Product Adoption

Feature coverage, module activation velocity, time-to-first-value, seat activation.

Financial

Invoice timeliness, failed charge streaks, discount dependency, ARPU delta.

Support

Ticket sentiment, reopen rate, SLA breaches, escalation frequency.

Social Proof

Referrals, NPS trend, review velocity, workspace invites.

Example Features

  • Engagement momentum: rolling 7/30-day activity ratio with exponential decay.
  • Adoption depth: % of key features used at least twice in last 14 days.
  • Payment health: days since last failed payment; dunning outcome streaks.
  • Collaboration: invites sent/accepted, shared artifacts, shared dashboards.
  • Support friction: avg. first-response time vs. SLA; negative sentiment count.
  • Value realization: time-to-first-key-event and time-to-second-key-event.

Normalization & Leakage Guardrails

  • Per-cohort normalization (plan, geo, device) to prevent bias.
  • Drop leakage features (future-looking flags, cancellations) during training.
  • Clip extreme monetary values; log-scale monetary features.
  • Impute with cohort medians; track null rates for drift.

Operationalization

  • Feast feature store with offline (BigQuery) + online (Redis) parity.
  • Feature freshness SLA: 15 minutes for hot signals, 24h for cold.
  • Automated validation: null checks, range checks, PSI drift alerts.
  • Versioned feature views; backward-compatible schemas.

Results

This feature set improved recall by 17 points at the same precision, increased churn lead time by 9 days, and reduced incentive spend by targeting only high-risk, high-LTV cohorts.
