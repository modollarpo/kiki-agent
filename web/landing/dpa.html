1. Introduction and Scope

1.1 Purpose

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between you ("Customer," "Controller," or "you") and KIKI, Inc. ("KIKI," "Processor," "we," or "us"). This DPA governs the processing of Personal Data by KIKI on behalf of Customer in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), UK GDPR, and other applicable data protection laws.

1.2 Application

This DPA applies when and to the extent that KIKI processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer as a Processor in the course of providing the Service. This DPA supplements the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

1.3 Order of Precedence

In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between this DPA and the Terms of Service, this DPA shall prevail to the extent of such conflict with respect to the processing of Personal Data.

1.4 Incorporation by Reference

By using the Service to process Personal Data, Customer agrees to this DPA. This DPA incorporates:

  • The Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission (2021 version) ‚Äì see Annex 1
  • Details of processing activities ‚Äì see Annex 2
  • Technical and organizational security measures ‚Äì see Annex 3
  • List of Sub-Processors ‚Äì see Annex 4

2. Definitions

Unless otherwise defined in this DPA, capitalized terms have the meanings set forth in the GDPR or the Terms of Service. The following additional definitions apply:

  • "Controller" means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data (i.e., Customer).
  • "Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws and regulations relating to privacy and data protection, including GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable laws.
  • "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed.
  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined in Article 4(1) GDPR.
  • "Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.
  • "Processing" means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, as defined in Article 4(2) GDPR.
  • "Processor" means an entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller (i.e., KIKI).
  • "Standard Contractual Clauses" or "SCCs" means the European Commission's standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries (2021 version).
  • "Sub-Processor" means any third party engaged by KIKI to process Personal Data on behalf of Customer.
  • "Supervisory Authority" means an independent public authority established by an EU Member State pursuant to GDPR Article 51.

3. Roles and Responsibilities

3.1 Role as Processor

KIKI acts as a Processor with respect to Personal Data processed through the Service. Customer acts as the Controller (or Processor if processing on behalf of another Controller). KIKI will process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer, except where required by applicable law (Article 28(3)(a) GDPR).

3.2 Controller's Obligations

Customer, as Controller, is responsible for:

  • Ensuring it has a lawful basis for processing Personal Data under applicable Data Protection Laws
  • Providing clear processing instructions to KIKI
  • Ensuring Personal Data is accurate, adequate, and limited to what is necessary
  • Obtaining all necessary consents from Data Subjects
  • Complying with Data Subject rights requests
  • Conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) where required
  • Maintaining records of processing activities as required by Article 30 GDPR
  • Notifying Supervisory Authorities and Data Subjects of breaches where required

3.3 Processor's Obligations

KIKI, as Processor, commits to:

  • Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer (Article 28(3)(a))
  • Ensure persons authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations (Article 28(3)(b))
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures (Article 28(3)(c), Article 32)
  • Engage Sub-Processors only with Customer's authorization (Article 28(3)(d))
  • Assist Customer in responding to Data Subject rights requests (Article 28(3)(e))
  • Assist Customer with security, breach notification, and impact assessments (Article 28(3)(f))
  • Delete or return Personal Data upon termination (Article 28(3)(g))
  • Make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow audits (Article 28(3)(h))
  • Immediately inform Customer if an instruction violates Data Protection Laws (Article 28(3))

4. Processing Instructions

4.1 Scope of Instructions

Customer instructs KIKI to process Personal Data to provide the Service as described in the Terms of Service and this DPA. Processing instructions include:

  • Operating and maintaining the KIKI platform
  • Processing data for customer retention analytics, LTV prediction, and engagement automation
  • Storing and backing up Personal Data
  • Providing customer support and troubleshooting
  • Improving the Service using aggregated, anonymized data
  • Complying with legal obligations (e.g., law enforcement requests with proper legal basis)

4.2 Changes to Instructions

Customer may issue additional written processing instructions via:

  • The Service's administrative interface and settings
  • Email to dpo@kiki.com
  • Amendments to the Order Form or service agreement

If KIKI believes an instruction violates Data Protection Laws, KIKI will immediately inform Customer and may suspend execution of the instruction until Customer confirms or modifies it.

4.3 Processing for Other Purposes

KIKI will not process Personal Data for purposes other than those instructed by Customer, except:

  • Where required by EU or Member State law (in which case KIKI will inform Customer before processing, unless prohibited by law)
  • To create aggregated, anonymized analytics that cannot be used to identify individuals (such data is not Personal Data)

5. Security Measures

5.1 Technical and Organizational Measures

KIKI has implemented and will maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access (Article 32 GDPR). Detailed security measures are set forth in Annex 3 and include:

Technical Measures:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.3 encryption in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC), multi-factor authentication (MFA), principle of least privilege
  • Network Security: Web application firewall (WAF), intrusion detection/prevention (IDS/IPS), DDoS protection
  • Infrastructure Security: SOC 2 Type II certified data centers (AWS, GCP), network segmentation, automated patching
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring, automated threat detection, security information and event management (SIEM)
  • Vulnerability Management: Regular penetration testing, automated vulnerability scanning, secure development lifecycle (SDL)

Organizational Measures:

  • Policies and Procedures: Information security policy, incident response plan, business continuity plan
  • Personnel Security: Background checks for employees with data access, mandatory security training, confidentiality agreements
  • Data Minimization: Collection and retention limited to what is necessary for the Service
  • Backup and Recovery: Daily automated backups, 90-day retention, tested disaster recovery procedures
  • Physical Security: Third-party data centers with 24/7 monitoring, badge access, CCTV surveillance
  • Certifications: SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, annual third-party audits

5.2 Security Updates

KIKI regularly reviews and updates security measures to ensure they remain appropriate and effective. Material changes to security measures will be documented and made available to Customer upon request.

5.3 Security Assessments

KIKI conducts:

  • Annual SOC 2 Type II audits by independent third-party auditors
  • Annual penetration testing by certified security firms
  • Quarterly vulnerability assessments and remediation
  • Continuous automated security monitoring

6. Sub-Processors

6.1 Authorization

Customer provides general authorization for KIKI to engage Sub-Processors to assist in providing the Service, subject to the conditions in this Section 6. A current list of Sub-Processors is set forth in Annex 4 and available at https://kiki.com/trust/sub-processors.

6.2 Sub-Processor Obligations

KIKI will:

  • Enter into a written agreement with each Sub-Processor imposing data protection obligations equivalent to this DPA
  • Ensure Sub-Processors provide appropriate security measures
  • Remain fully liable to Customer for the performance of Sub-Processors' obligations
  • Conduct due diligence on Sub-Processors before engagement
  • Monitor Sub-Processor compliance on an ongoing basis

6.3 Notice of New Sub-Processors

KIKI will provide Customer with at least 30 days' prior written notice of any intended addition or replacement of Sub-Processors by:

  • Email notification to the Customer's account administrator
  • Updates to the Sub-Processor list at https://kiki.com/trust/sub-processors

6.4 Objection to Sub-Processors

Customer may object to the appointment of a new Sub-Processor on reasonable data protection grounds by notifying KIKI within 15 days of receiving notice. If Customer objects:

  • KIKI will use reasonable efforts to make available a change to the Service or recommend a commercially reasonable alternative
  • If no alternative is available, Customer may terminate the affected Service and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees for the terminated portion

7. Data Subject Rights

7.1 Assistance with Data Subject Requests

Taking into account the nature of the processing, KIKI will assist Customer by appropriate technical and organizational measures in fulfilling Customer's obligations to respond to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights under Data Protection Laws, including:

  • Right of Access (Article 15): KIKI will provide tools to export Personal Data in machine-readable format (JSON, CSV)
  • Right to Rectification (Article 16): Customer can update Personal Data via the Service interface
  • Right to Erasure (Article 17): KIKI will delete Personal Data upon Customer's request within 30 days
  • Right to Restriction (Article 18): KIKI will restrict processing upon Customer's instruction
  • Right to Data Portability (Article 20): KIKI provides export functionality in JSON and CSV formats
  • Right to Object (Article 21): Customer can configure processing preferences and opt-outs

7.2 Customer's Role

As Controller, Customer is responsible for:

  • Receiving and responding to Data Subject requests
  • Verifying the identity of Data Subjects making requests
  • Determining the validity and scope of requests
  • Providing instructions to KIKI as needed to fulfill requests

7.3 Direct Requests to KIKI

If KIKI receives a Data Subject request directly, KIKI will:

  • Promptly notify Customer of the request
  • Not respond to the request without Customer's prior written authorization (unless legally required to do so)
  • Redirect the Data Subject to submit the request to Customer

7.4 Fees for Assistance

KIKI will provide reasonable assistance at no additional charge. If a request requires excessive KIKI resources (e.g., responding to hundreds of individual requests), KIKI may charge a reasonable fee based on time and materials, with advance notice to Customer.

8. Data Breach Notification

8.1 Notification to Customer

KIKI will notify Customer without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer's Personal Data (Article 33 GDPR). Notification will be sent to Customer's designated security contact via email and the Service's administrative dashboard.

8.2 Breach Notification Contents

KIKI's breach notification will include, to the extent reasonably possible:

  • Description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records affected
  • Name and contact details of KIKI's Data Protection Officer or other contact point
  • Description of likely consequences of the breach
  • Description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its possible adverse effects
  • Timeline of events and discovery
  • Status of investigation and remediation efforts

8.3 Customer's Obligations

Customer is responsible for:

  • Assessing whether notification to Supervisory Authorities is required under Article 33 GDPR (within 72 hours of awareness)
  • Assessing whether notification to Data Subjects is required under Article 34 GDPR (without undue delay)
  • Complying with all applicable breach notification laws
  • Maintaining accurate security contact information in Customer's account

8.4 Cooperation

KIKI will reasonably cooperate with Customer's investigation and provide additional information as it becomes available. KIKI will not publicly disclose the breach without Customer's prior written consent, except as required by law.

9. Audits and Inspections

9.1 Audit Rights

KIKI will make available to Customer information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and applicable Data Protection Laws (Article 28(3)(h) GDPR). Customer may exercise audit rights by:

9.1.1 Standard Audit Reports:

  • KIKI will provide current SOC 2 Type II reports upon request (subject to confidentiality agreement)
  • ISO 27001 certificates available at https://kiki.com/trust
  • Third-party penetration test summaries (excluding detailed vulnerabilities)

9.1.2 Audit Questionnaires:

  • Customer may submit written security questionnaires up to twice per year
  • KIKI will respond within 30 days

9.1.3 On-Site Audits:

For Enterprise customers only, Customer may conduct on-site audits or inspections, subject to:

  • Reasonable advance written notice (at least 60 days)
  • Frequency limited to once per year (unless breach or Supervisory Authority request)
  • Conducted during business hours at mutually agreed times
  • Execution of KIKI's standard confidentiality agreement
  • Customer bears all costs associated with the audit
  • No disruption to KIKI's operations or other customers
  • Compliance with KIKI's security and confidentiality policies

9.2 Supervisory Authority Audits

If a Supervisory Authority requests an audit of KIKI's processing on behalf of Customer, KIKI will:

  • Promptly notify Customer of the request
  • Cooperate with the Supervisory Authority
  • Provide requested information and access (subject to legal limitations)

10. International Data Transfers

10.1 Data Residency Options

Customer may choose where Personal Data is stored and processed:

  • United States: AWS US-East (Virginia), US-West (Oregon)
  • European Union: AWS EU-Central (Frankfurt), EU-West (Ireland), GCP EU regions
  • United Kingdom: AWS EU-West (London)
  • Other regions: Canada (Montreal), Australia (Sydney), Japan (Tokyo)

10.2 Standard Contractual Clauses

For transfers of Personal Data from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to countries not recognized as providing adequate protection, the parties agree to be bound by the Standard Contractual Clauses (Module Two: Controller to Processor) approved by the European Commission Decision 2021/914, incorporated as Annex 1.

The SCCs apply as follows:

  • Module: Module Two (Controller to Processor)
  • Parties: Customer (data exporter/Controller), KIKI (data importer/Processor)
  • Docking Clause: Available (Clause 7)
  • Governing Law: Law of the EEA Member State where Customer is established (Clause 17)
  • Dispute Resolution: Courts of the EEA Member State where Customer is established (Clause 18)
  • Competent Supervisory Authority: Customer's lead Supervisory Authority (Clause 13)

10.3 Additional Safeguards

In addition to SCCs, KIKI implements supplementary measures for international transfers:

  • End-to-end encryption for data in transit and at rest
  • Strict access controls and authentication requirements
  • Regular security audits and certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
  • Contractual obligations requiring Sub-Processors to implement equivalent protections
  • Data minimization and pseudonymization where possible
  • Commitment to challenge disproportionate government access requests

10.4 UK and Swiss Transfers

For transfers subject to UK GDPR or Swiss data protection law:

  • UK: The UK Addendum to the SCCs (B.1.0) applies
  • Switzerland: The Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner's requirements apply

11. Deletion and Return of Data

11.1 Deletion Upon Termination

Upon termination or expiration of the Terms of Service, KIKI will:

  • Export Period: Provide a 30-day grace period for Customer to export Personal Data
  • Deletion Timeline: Delete all Personal Data within 90 days of termination
  • Secure Deletion: Use industry-standard secure deletion methods (e.g., NIST 800-88 guidelines)
  • Backup Deletion: Delete Personal Data from backups within 90 days
  • Certification: Provide written certification of deletion upon Customer's request

11.2 Exceptions to Deletion

KIKI may retain Personal Data to the extent required by applicable law (e.g., tax records, audit logs) or to the extent stored in backup systems during the regular backup retention period (maximum 90 days), provided such Personal Data is isolated and protected from further processing.

11.3 Data Export

Customer may export Personal Data at any time via:

  • Self-service export tools in the Service interface (JSON, CSV formats)
  • API access using Customer's API credentials
  • Request to support@kiki.com for bulk export assistance

11.4 Return Instead of Deletion

If Customer requests return of Personal Data instead of deletion, KIKI will return the data in a commonly used, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV) within 30 days of the request.

12. Liability and Indemnification

12.1 Liability Under This DPA

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the Terms of Service, except where Data Protection Laws prohibit such limitations.

12.2 GDPR Liability Allocation

Pursuant to Article 82 GDPR:

  • KIKI is liable only for damages caused by processing that violates GDPR obligations specific to Processors (Article 28) or where KIKI acted outside or contrary to lawful instructions from Customer
  • KIKI is not liable if it can prove it is not in any way responsible for the event giving rise to the damage
  • As between Customer and KIKI, Customer is responsible for compliance with Controller obligations under GDPR

12.3 Indemnification

KIKI will indemnify Customer for claims by Data Subjects or Supervisory Authorities arising from KIKI's breach of its obligations under this DPA, subject to:

  • Customer promptly notifying KIKI of the claim
  • KIKI having sole control of defense and settlement
  • Customer providing reasonable cooperation
  • Limitations in the Terms of Service (to the extent permitted by law)

13. Term and Termination

13.1 Term

This DPA enters into force on the date Customer first uses the Service to process Personal Data and remains in effect for as long as KIKI processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer.

13.2 Effect of Termination

Termination of the Terms of Service automatically terminates this DPA, subject to:

  • KIKI's obligations to delete or return Personal Data (Section 11)
  • Survival of confidentiality and liability provisions
  • Retention of data required by law

13.3 Survival

The following provisions survive termination: Sections 5 (Security), 8 (Data Breach), 9 (Audits), 11 (Deletion), 12 (Liability), and any provisions of the SCCs that expressly survive termination.

Annex 3: Technical and Organizational Security Measures

KIKI implements the following security measures pursuant to Article 32 GDPR. For complete details, see Trust Center.

A. Measures of Pseudonymization and Encryption

  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for data in transit
  • Encrypted database connections
  • Tokenization of payment card data
  • Hashed and salted passwords (bcrypt)
  • End-to-end encryption for sensitive communications

B. Measures to Ensure Confidentiality

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with principle of least privilege
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) mandatory for all users
  • Unique user accounts (no shared credentials)
  • Automated session timeouts
  • Confidentiality agreements with all employees and contractors
  • Background checks for personnel with data access
  • Annual security awareness training

C. Measures to Ensure Integrity

  • Automated data integrity checks and checksums
  • Version control and audit trails for data modifications
  • Input validation and sanitization
  • Change management procedures
  • Regular data quality monitoring

D. Measures to Ensure Availability and Resilience

  • 99.99% uptime SLA with redundant infrastructure
  • Multi-region deployment with failover capabilities
  • Daily automated backups with 90-day retention
  • Disaster recovery plan (RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 1 hour)
  • DDoS protection and traffic filtering
  • 24/7 system monitoring and alerting
  • Redundant power, cooling, and network connectivity in data centers

E. Measures for Regular Testing and Evaluation

  • Annual SOC 2 Type II audits
  • Annual third-party penetration testing
  • Quarterly vulnerability scans
  • Continuous automated security testing (SAST/DAST)
  • Regular disaster recovery and backup restoration testing
  • Incident response plan reviews and tabletop exercises

F. Incident Response and Management

  • 24/7 security operations center (SOC)
  • Documented incident response plan
  • Security information and event management (SIEM) system
  • Automated threat detection and response
  • Breach notification procedures (72-hour timeline)
  • Post-incident review and remediation process

G. Physical Security (Third-Party Data Centers)

  • SOC 2 Type II certified facilities (AWS, GCP)
  • 24/7 on-site security personnel
  • Biometric and badge-controlled access
  • CCTV surveillance
  • Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control)
